Kahoot Bot

Back when I was in middle school, there were a variety of free-to-use Kahoot bots on the Chrome web store and on various websites that would flood a game with dozens and dozens of bot players. By the time high school rolled around, one bot creator decided to monetize his/her product, and soon enough, everyone tagged along. Without any free options to troll my classes with, I decided to create a simple, concise, and inconspicuous Kahoot bot with Python. With the power of the selenium webdriver API, it's less than 50 lines of code!

Price Scraper

As an avid Old School RuneScape player, monitoring the economy is always on my mind. Whether it's buying a new best-in-slot piece of gear, or just flipping some resources, price tracking is always involved. Looking to invest in some very expensive items in-game, I wanted to know by what percentage each item dropped/increased every day. Using the BeautifulSoup web scraper in conjunction with Google's spreadsheet API, I was able to automate price checks and analyze the price trends for my own investment decisions.

Zulrah GUI

The Zulrah GUI is the product of my slight frustrations with Zulrah helpers for the Old School RuneScape boss, Zulrah. Though they functioned fine as GUIs, they didn't include a few crucial details about the boss fight's phases. Having already created my own rotation diagrams for the boss fight, I decided to create my own solution to this problem. I did some research on Python GUIs and ended up utilizing the Tkinter GUI API to create a fully functioning GUI that would show individual rotations depending on the in-game rotations of the boss fight.
